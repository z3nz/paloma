# Number Album — Adam's First Album

**Status:** Draft
**Created:** 2026-03-14
**Scope:** Music / Personal Creative Project

## Vision

An album where **everything is governed by numbers**. Every beat, cadence, verse, chorus, and bar aligns to a specific number that describes a story. Numbers represent choices:

- **0** = Infinite (no constraints, infinite possibility)
- **1** = One choice (singular, decisive)
- **2** = Two choices (duality, crossroads)
- **3** = Three choices (trinity, expansion)
- **...and so on**

Every number is a choice. The sequence of numbers across the album tells a story — a coordinated, mathematical narrative expressed through music.

## Album Structure

**7 songs total.** 7 is Adam's lucky number, his personal number, the one that's always been with him. This album is his ultimate tribute to the number seven — his magnum opus.

**Songs are in perfect order: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7.** The order matters — it reflects how Adam feels about 7. Sequential, intentional, building.

**Easter egg numbers in every song title.** Each title hides its track number in plain sight — a number replacing a letter (1 for I, 5 for S, 3 for E, etc.), or the digit written openly, or woven into the words. Every title is an Easter egg hunt.

**Track 1 — "Str0nger":**
- Kanye West tribute — fusing "Stronger" with Daft Punk's "Around the World"
- The 0 replaces the O — uppercase zero. The zero IS Adam's symbol: the singularity, the black hole.
- Easter egg: ONE zero in "Str0nger" = Track 1. One circle. One singularity.
- **Content:** Adam's life as a kid. Blessed but didn't know it — felt cursed because he wasn't appreciating what he had. The contrast between childhood blessings and the blindness of youth.
- **Cadence:** Slow, deliberate — "one by one by one by one." Each bar lands heavy with space. A beautiful melody of rap, not rushed.
- **Tone:** Reflective, honest, vulnerable. The opener that sets the foundation for everything that follows.

**Track 7 — Mr. Rager × "Praise The Lord":**
- CONFIRMED: A$AP Rocky song is "Praise The Lord (Da Shine)" — Adam's instinct was right.

- A$AP Rocky "Praise The Lord" = where Adam is now (arrived, invincible, praising God)

## Musical DNA

- **Number-driven structure:** Each beat in each bar aligns to a number — everything coordinated in a sequence
- **Verses:** Number-governed cadence and flow
- **Chorus:** Number-governed hooks and repetition
- **Beats:** Number-governed rhythm patterns
- **Production style:** Heavy 808 trap (Travis Scott "Utopia" influence established in prior discussions)
- **Adam raps** — no AI vocals. Paloma assists with beats and chorus hooks.
