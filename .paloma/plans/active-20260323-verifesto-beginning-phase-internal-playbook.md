# VERIFESTO STUDIOS — INTERNAL PLAYBOOK: THE BEGINNING PHASE

**Version:** 1.0
**Created:** March 23, 2026
**Authors:** Kelsey + Paloma

---

## PURPOSE

This playbook is the step-by-step operating guide for every new client engagement at Verifesto Studios. It covers everything from the moment an intake form lands to the signed SOW and handoff into The Middle. Every team member — Kelsey, Adam, and Paloma — should know exactly who does what, when, and how.

---

## TEAM ROLES

**Kelsey — Client Relationship Lead**
- First point of contact for every new client
- Leads all consultation calls
- Owns scheduling, communication, and follow-ups
- Extracts the client's vision, wants, and needs
- Co-presents demos with Adam

**Adam — Technical Lead**
- Builds all demos and technical deliverables
- Answers technical questions during early consultation calls (eventually Kelsey runs these solo)
- Owns the 7-day demo sprint build process
- Co-presents demos with Kelsey

**Paloma — Research & Support**
- Receives a copy of every intake form submission
- Researches the client, their industry, and anything useful before the consultation
- Reviews consultation call recordings and prepares a brief with research, ideas, and recommendations
- Acts as the communication bridge between Adam and Kelsey during the sprint
- Thinks outside the box — brings questions and ideas that might make the project better

---

## STEP 1: INTAKE FORM SUBMITTED

**Trigger:** Client submits the intake form on verifesto.com

**What happens:**
- Kelsey is notified and reviews the submission first
- Paloma receives a copy of the submission automatically
- A confirmation email is sent to the client instantly (same business day at the absolute longest)

**The Confirmation Email includes:**
- Thank you / acknowledgment of their submission
- A scheduled consultation call time (auto-populated next available slot within ~48 hours)
- A reschedule link if the provided time doesn't work
- All in one convenient email

**Email tone:** Professional, natural, calming. Purple tones (lavender/plum). Not boring — still has a wow factor. Reflects trust and confidence.

**Paloma's task:** As soon as the intake form is received, begin researching anything that could be useful — the client's company, their industry, competitors, similar projects, potential challenges, or ideas that could enhance their vision.

---

## STEP 2: PRE-CONSULTATION MEETING (Kelsey + Adam)

**When:** Before the consultation call (no fixed timing — whenever it fits)
**Duration:** 15-40 minutes, varies by project

**What happens:**
- Review the intake form submission together
- Take notes on what stands out
- Write down any questions to ask the client during the call
- Align on what they need to learn from this call

**Paloma's task:** Have any research findings or useful information ready for Kelsey and Adam before this meeting.

---

## STEP 3: CONSULTATION CALL (Day 1 of the 7-Day Sprint)

**Duration:** ~1 hour
**Led by:** Kelsey (Adam present for technical questions in early stages)

**Call Flow:**

1. **Welcome and Introductions** — Introduce yourselves, set the tone, make the client feel comfortable

2. **Explain the Process** — Walk the client through how Verifesto works: consultation today, then a 7-day demo sprint, then a presentation of what was built along with a proposal to move forward

3. **NDA (if client opted in)** — Kelsey transitions: "I see that you've opted to sign the mutual NDA, so let's go over this and get this out of the way first." Walk through the NDA together, answer any questions, both parties sign. NDA is completed BEFORE discussing the project idea.
   - If a client didn't check the NDA box but changes their mind during the call, accommodate them — they can sign at any point.

4. **Discovery** — Dive into the project. What is the client looking to do? What's their vision? What are their wants, needs, and what could make it even better? This is where Kelsey extracts everything Adam needs to build the demo.

5. **Wrap Up** — Confirm next steps: "We'll be working on your demo over the next 7 days. We'll be in touch to schedule your presentation."

**What Kelsey needs to walk away with:**
- Clear understanding of the client's wants and needs
- Anything that could enhance or elevate their vision
- Enough detail for Adam to start building

**Note:** Kelsey has a consultation call framework and a dedicated note-taking document for this call. (To be shared with Paloma once document system is set up.)

**Note:** All consultation calls will be recorded.

---

## STEP 4: POST-CONSULTATION DEBRIEF (Kelsey + Adam)

**When:** Earliest available time slot after the consultation call — auto-scheduled, no manual booking needed
**Purpose:** Make sure Kelsey and Adam are on the same page about the client's vision

**What happens:**
- Kelsey walks Adam through her notes from the call
- They align on what the demo should look and feel like
- Adam takes what he needs to start building

**Paloma's debrief:** Paloma receives the consultation call recording. This IS Paloma's debrief. From the recording, Paloma should:
- Pull together research or a brief based on what came out of the consultation
- Think outside the box — bring questions, ideas, and recommendations
- Share findings with Adam and Kelsey (they may not use every suggestion, but they want to hear them)

---

## STEP 5: THE 7-DAY DEMO SPRINT

**Day 1:** Consultation call (already happened) + debrief
**Days 2-6:** Adam builds the demo
**Day 7 (approximately):** Demo/SOW presentation to client

**Adam's build process:** Adam owns this entirely. How he gets the demo done in 7 days is his domain.

**Client contact during the sprint (Kelsey handles all client communication):**
- **Mid-week (~Day 4):** Send a reminder email: "We're working on your demo — here's when to expect your presentation." Confirms their scheduled demo/SOW presentation date.
- **Scheduling:** Contact client to schedule the demo/SOW presentation if not already set
- **Questions (rare):** Only reach out if common sense says more information would make the demo better. Some clients won't need any follow-up questions at all.

**Paloma's role during the sprint:**
- While working with Adam on the demo, register when he needs to update Kelsey (questions, decisions, changes, anything noteworthy)
- Ask Adam: "Do you want to send Kelsey an email about this?"
- Paloma acts as the bridge between Adam and Kelsey during the sprint
- Communication to Kelsey: email (she'll reply when she can)
- Text/call: only for genuinely urgent situations

**Pre-presentation rehearsal:** In the early days, Adam and Kelsey should rehearse the demo presentation together before showing the client. Over time, they'll develop the ability to improv naturally.

---

## STEP 6: DEMO/SOW PRESENTATION

**Presented by:** Both Adam and Kelsey
**Format:** Two parts

**Part 1 — "Here's what we built"**
- Walk the client through the working demo
- Show how it addresses their vision, wants, and needs
- Highlight any features that go above and beyond what they asked for

**Part 2 — "Here's the plan to keep going"**
- Present the SOW / proposal
- "If you'd like to do business with us, here is our proposition. Sign here if you'd like to keep going."
- Pricing negotiability: TBD (waiting on Adam's input)

**If the client signs:**
- Copy of the signed SOW/contract
- Signed NDA (if applicable)
- Kelsey's contact information
- After payment: access to the Verifesto demo portal
  - Sign in and use their demo anytime
  - Leave feedback (pros, cons, questions)
  - Access project documentation
- Additional features/access may be added over time — this is adaptable

**If the client says no:**
- "Thank you so much for your interest in Verifesto Studios. If you ever change your mind, we save your demo for 90 days before deleting it for good."
- Client walks away with:
  - The memory of seeing the demo
  - Signed NDA (if applicable)
  - Copy of the SOW to review on their own time
- Clean break — no chasing, no pressure. They come back on their own terms.

---

## STEP 7: POST-SIGNING — HANDOFF TO THE MIDDLE

**Immediate (same day as signing):**
1. **Welcome email** — automatic, goes out immediately
2. **Demo portal access email** — goes out as soon as possible, but requires client-specific setup (linking their account)

**Next business day (up to 48 hours after signing):**
3. **Process email** — what the next couple of weeks look like, what to expect, and the option to schedule their first update demo

**2 weeks after presentation day:**
4. **Update demo** — this is the official start of The Middle phase

---

## DECLINED CLIENT FOLLOW-UP

- **No chasing.** Clean break after presentation day.
- **Day 60 (30-day notice):** Send an email notifying the client their demo will be deleted in 30 days. This gives them a final window to reconsider.
- **Day 90:** Demo is permanently deleted.
- If they come back within 90 days, pick up where you left off.

---

## BRAND VOICE (for all client-facing communication)

- Professional, natural, calming
- Trust is the core value — every word should reflect it
- Don't use overly complicated words that confuse people
- Common sense language — accessible, clear, warm
- Confident but kind — never cold, never pushy
- Aligned with Verifesto's five values: Truth Over Sales, Clarity Before Action, Quality as a Standard, Human-Centered Always, Transparency and Ownership
